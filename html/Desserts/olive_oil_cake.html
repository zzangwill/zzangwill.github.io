Olive Oil Cake
Recipe from: New York Times
Makes: 1 Nine-Inch Cake
  • 1 cup/240mL extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 cups/225g flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 1/2 cup/300g sugar
  • 3 large eggs at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon freshly grated lemon zest plus 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 1/4 cup/295mL whole milk at room temperature

Preheat oven to 375 degrees and grease a 9-inch pan with olive oil, lining it with parchment paper. Oil the parchment paper and flour the pan, shaking out any excess flour.

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, baking powder and akign soda.

In the bowl of an electric mixer set on high, beat the sugar, egs, and lemon zest until very thick and fluffly, about 5 minutes. With the mixer still running, slowly drizzle in the oil and beat until incorporated, about 2 more minutes. Reduce speed to low, and add milk and lemon juice. Gradually add the flour mixture and beat until just combined. Transfer the batter to the prepared pan and sprinkle the top with 2 tablespoons sugar.

Bake the cake until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean, 40-45 minutes. Transfer to a rack and let it cool for 20 minutes, and the release it from the pan. Invert the cake and flip it over to cool even more.
