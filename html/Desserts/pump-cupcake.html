Pumpkin Cupcakes with Cream Cheese Frosting
Recipe from: Baking DeCal
Makes: 12 cupcakes

Frosting:

  • 8 oz full-fat block of cream cheese, softened to room temperature (I believe this is 1 block)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 3 cups confectioner's sugar, plus an extra 1/4 cup if needed
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1/8 tsp salt

Cupcakes:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tso pure vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 tsp pumpkin pie spice (combine cinnamon, ginger, cloves)
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Line a 12-cup muffin pan with cupcake liners.

Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and pumpkin spice together in a a large bowl. Set aside. Whisk the oil, eggs, brown sugar, pumpkin, and vanilla together until combined. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and use a mixer of whisk until completely combined. Batter will be thick.

Pour/spoon the batter into the liners- - fill only 2/3 full to avoid spilling over the sides. Bake for 20-22 min, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. For around 30 mini cupcakes, bake for 12-13 minutes, at the same temperature. Allow the cupcakes to cool compeltely before frosting.

Make the frosting: in a large bowl using a handheld or stand mizer fitted with a paddle or whisk attachment, beat the cream cheese and butter together on high speed until smooth and creamy. Add 3 tbp confectioner's sugar, vanilla, and salt. Beat on low speed for 30 seconds, then switch to a hgih speed and beat for 2 minutes. If you want the frosting a little thicker, add the extra 1/4 cup of confectioner's sugar.

Frost cooled cupcakes however you like. Store leftovers for up to 5 days in the fridge.
