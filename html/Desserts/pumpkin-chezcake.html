Salted Caramel Pumpkin Cheesecake
Recipe from: Baking DeCal
Makes: 16 cupcakes

Gingersnap Crust:

  • 1 and 1/4 cup gingersnap cookie crumbs grounded/crumbled (ie Nabisco)
  • 2 tbs granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalt3ed butter

Topping:

  • 1 cup caramel sauce
  • 1/2 cup chopped becans (toast for 10 min at 300 degrees)

Filling:

  • 12 oz brick-style cream cheese, softened to room temperature (I think this is 2 blocks)
  • 1/ cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 large egg + 1 large egg yolk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Line a 12-count muffin pan, and a second one with 4 lined.

Make the crust: mix the cookie crumbs, sugar, and butter together in a medium bowl until combined. The mixture will b e thick, coarse, and sandy. Press a heaping tablespoon of mixture down into each liner, making sure the crust is tight and compact. You might have a little crust leftover. If so, press a little more into each liner - if desired! Prebake the crusts for 5 minutes. Remove from teh oven and set aside.

Make the filling: in a large bowl using a handheld or stand mixer with a whisk attachment, beat the cream cehese on high speed until completely smooth (about 1 minute). On a medium-high speed, beat in pumpkin, egg, and egg yolk, scraping down sides of the bowl as needed. On high speed, beat in sugar, cinnamon, pumpkin spice, and vanilla until combined.

Pour the filling evenly into each crust. Bake until centers only slightly jiggle, 18-20 minutesl Let the cheesecake cool at room temperature in the pan set on a wire rack. Once completely cool, place cheesecakes in the fridge for at least 2 hours or overnight. The cheesecakes will slightly sink in the middle - that's okay!

Once chilled, drizzle each with salted caramel sauce and garnish with pecans. Store leftovers covered tightly in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
