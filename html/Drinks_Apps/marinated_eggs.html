Korean Marinated Eggs
Recipe from: Tiffy Cooks
Serves: depends on how eggie you feel

Eggs:

  • 12 eggs
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp vinegar

Marinade:

  • 6 cloves Garlic minced
  • 2 Korean Green Pepper chopped
  • 1 Red Chili chopped - optional
  • 3 Green Onion chopped - optional
  • 1 tbsp Sesame Seeds
  • 1 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1/2 cup Honey
  • 1 tbsp Sesame Oil
  • 1/2 cup Water

In a pot, add in water (enough to cover all the eggs) and add salt and vinegar. Turn the heat up to medium-high and bring the water to a simmer. Once the water is boiling, carefully add in the eggs and let them cook for 6 minutes.

After 6 minutes, remove eggs and let it rest in a bowl of ice water for at least 10 minutes. Peel carefully and set them aside.

In a container, add garlic, chopped green pepper, red chili, green onion, soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, and water. Mix till everything is combined. Add in the peel eggs and making sure the eggs are covered in the marinade. Let the eggs rest in the fridge for at least 2 hours or better overnight. When hungry, take one out, service in a bowl of hot rice and furikake, and enjoy!
