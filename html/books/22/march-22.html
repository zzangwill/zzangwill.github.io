March Reads and Reflections:

Prince of Throns by Mark Lawrence - 2-3/5 stars

  • 2-3 stars, I’m kind of torn. From reading several other reviews, I don’t think anything I’m about to type is an original thought so I’ll (try to) keep it short. I read this book since I was desiring a book with a dark protagonist. Boy, did the author deliver on that front. I don’t think “dark” accurately describes Jorg — he’s cruel, twisted, and obsessive. Therefore, props on that front. Further props on an interesting world. The twist revealed I didn’t find shocking, it instead allowed pieces to come together the author had planted. I also enjoyed the authors writing style, as well as a nice selection of one-liners that stood out as very poetic. Downfalls: Yay more sexual violence (rape) against women (and regular violence) Yay kill off the token black man that never even gets a name. Yay 14 year old that someone exerts his dominance over a group of older men with a level of emotional, intellectual, and sexual maturity, that to me, makes no sense for him to possess. For me, the cons weight heavier than the pros, therefore I am at a 2-3/5 but not truly settled on which. From reading reviews, I am unsure on if I will read the sequel, but I am definitely not counting it out as a possibility in the future.
