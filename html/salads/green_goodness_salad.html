Green Goodness Salad
Recipe from: Ian's Cookbook
Serves: 1
  • 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast, trimmed
  • 1/4 teaspoon table salt, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper, divided
  • 2 teaspoon oil
  • 3 tablespoons water, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated lemon zest + 1 1/2 teaspoons juice
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley or chives
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh tarragon
  • 4 ounces (4 cups) baby spinach
  • 1/4 cup frozen shelled edamame, thawed and patted dry
  • 2 tablespoons chopped toasted pistachios

Place chicken on cutting board and cover with plastic wrap. Using meat pounder, gently pound chicken to even thickness. Pat chicken dry with paper towels and sprinkle with 1/8 teaspoon salt and 1/8 teaspoon pepper. Heat oil in 8 or 10 inch skillet over medium heat until just smoking. Brown chicken well on first side, about 3 minutes.

Flip chicken, add 2 tablespoons water, and cover skillet. Reduce heat to low and continue to cook until chicken registers 160 degrees, about 3 minutes. Transfer chicken to plate and let cool slightly. Using 2 forks, shred chicken into bite-sized pieces.

Combine lemon zest and juice and garlic in medium bowl and let sit for 5 minutes. Stir in yogurt, remaining 1 tablespoon water, parsley, tarragon, remaining 1/8 teaspoon salt, and remaining 1/8 teaspoon pepper; set aside.

Toss spinach with half of drsesing to coat, then season with salt and pepper to taste. Top with shredded chicken, edameme, avocado, and pistachios and drizzle with remaining dressing. Serve. Salad and vinaigrette can be refrigerated seperately for up to 2 days.
